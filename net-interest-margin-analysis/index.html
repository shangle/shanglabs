Net Interest Margin Analysis Help

What Is This Tool?

The NIM Analysis tool calculates and tracks Net Interest Margin, the key profitability metric for banks. It breaks down interest income and expense components to show how effectively a bank manages its assets and liabilities.

Getting Started

  1. Enter Income Components -- Input Interest Income, Non-Interest Income, and Other Income in thousands.
  2. Enter Expense Components -- Input Interest Expense and Operating Expenses.
  3. Enter Balance Sheet Data -- Provide Total Earning Assets and Total Assets.
  4. View Results -- Calculations update automatically as you change inputs.

Key Metrics

  • Net Interest Margin (NIM): (Interest Income - Interest Expense) / Average Earning Assets. The core measure of bank profitability from lending activities.
  • Net Interest Income: The absolute dollar difference between interest earned and interest paid.
  • Yield on Earning Assets: Interest Income / Earning Assets. Shows the return on the loan and investment portfolio.
  • Cost of Funds: Interest Expense / Total Assets. Shows the average cost of deposits and borrowings.
  • Efficiency Ratio: Operating Expenses / Total Revenue. Lower is better; measures operational efficiency.

Industry Benchmarks

  • Community Banks: NIM typically 3.0-4.0%
  • Large Banks: NIM typically 2.5-3.5%
  • Credit Unions: NIM typically 2.5-3.5%
Tip: A declining NIM trend often signals competitive pricing pressure or a shifting asset mix. Compare your NIM against peer institutions to gauge relative performance.

Export Options

  • PDF: Formatted report suitable for ALCO or board presentations.
  • CSV: Raw data for further analysis in spreadsheets.
Note: NIM is sensitive to interest rate changes. Consider running scenarios with different rate assumptions to understand your institution's interest rate sensitivity.
