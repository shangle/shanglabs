Payment Reconciliation Help

What Is This Tool?

The Payment Reconciliation Helper automatically matches incoming payments to outstanding invoices. Upload your payments and invoices data to find matches, identify discrepancies, and resolve unmatched items.

Getting Started

  1. Upload Payments CSV -- File with payment records including payment ID, amount, date, and reference.
  2. Upload Invoices CSV -- File with invoice records including invoice ID, amount, date, and customer.
  3. Configure Matching -- Set the matching tolerance (how close amounts need to be to auto-match).
  4. Run Reconciliation -- Click "Reconcile" to automatically match payments to invoices.

Matching Methods

Exact Match: Amount and reference match perfectly
Fuzzy Match: Amounts within tolerance range
Manual Match: User-driven pairing
Batch Match: Multiple items at once

Understanding Results

  • Matched: Payment successfully paired with an invoice. Green status.
  • Partial Match: Payment covers only part of an invoice amount. Yellow status.
  • Unmatched Payments: Payments that don't correspond to any invoice. May be overpayments or advance payments.
  • Unmatched Invoices: Invoices without a corresponding payment. These are still outstanding.

Manual Matching

For items the auto-matcher can't resolve, use the manual matching interface to pair payments and invoices yourself. This is useful for partial payments, combined payments, or cases where the reference doesn't match exactly.

Tip: Click column headers in the results table to sort by that column. This makes it easy to group similar items and spot patterns in unmatched records.

Export

Export generates a full reconciliation report including matched pairs, unmatched items, and a summary of the reconciliation status. Use this for audit trails and month-end close procedures.

Note: All matching is done in your browser. No payment or invoice data is sent to any server. Your financial data stays private.
