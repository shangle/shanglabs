3D Connector Builder Help

What Is This Tool?

The 3D Connector Builder generates printable STL files for custom connection adapters. Design pipe connectors, tube adapters, and junction pieces with precise measurements, then download the STL for your 3D printer.

Getting Started

  1. Select Connection Type -- Choose from Straight, T-Junction, Y-Junction, Cross, Elbow, or Reducer.
  2. Set Dimensions -- Adjust inner diameter, outer diameter, length, and wall thickness.
  3. Preview -- Use the 3D preview to rotate and inspect the connector from all angles.
  4. Download STL -- Click the download button to get the printable STL file.

Connection Types

Straight: Simple tube connector
T-Junction: 90-degree branch
Y-Junction: Angled split
Cross: 4-way intersection
Elbow: 90-degree bend
Reducer: Size transition

Dimension Parameters

  • Inner Diameter: The inside measurement of the connector. Match this to the outer diameter of the tubes you're connecting.
  • Outer Diameter: The outside measurement. Determines overall connector size and strength.
  • Length: How long each arm of the connector extends.
  • Wall Thickness: Difference between outer and inner diameter. Thicker walls provide more strength.

3D Preview Controls

  • Rotate: Click and drag to rotate the model.
  • Zoom: Scroll wheel to zoom in/out.
  • Pan: Right-click and drag to pan.
Tip: Always add 0.2-0.5mm tolerance to your inner diameter for a friction fit, depending on your printer's accuracy. Test with a short sample print first.

Printing Recommendations

  • Layer Height: 0.2mm for standard, 0.1mm for high precision
  • Infill: 20-40% for functional parts
  • Material: PLA for prototypes, PETG or ABS for functional connectors
  • Supports: May be needed for elbow and Y-junction types
Note: The generated STL is a simplified preview geometry. For production or high-stress applications, consider refining the model in a CAD program like Fusion 360 or FreeCAD.
