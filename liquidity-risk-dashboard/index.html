Liquidity Risk Dashboard Help

What Is This Tool?

The Liquidity Risk Dashboard monitors a bank's ability to meet short-term obligations. It tracks key liquidity ratios, cash positions, and provides early warning indicators when liquidity buffers are thinning.

Key Metrics Explained

  • Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR): High Quality Liquid Assets divided by Net Cash Outflows over 30 days. Must be above 100% per Basel III requirements.
  • Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR): Available Stable Funding divided by Required Stable Funding. Must be above 100%. Measures long-term funding stability.
  • Cash Position: Current cash and cash equivalents available. Monitor month-over-month trends.
  • Liquidity Gap: Projected shortfall or surplus over the next 30 days. A negative gap requires management attention.
  • Daily Cash Flow: Average net daily cash position. Helps identify daily liquidity patterns.
  • 30-Day Outflows: Total projected cash outflows for the next month.

Understanding the Charts

  • Liquidity Trend: Shows LCR and NSFR over time. Look for downward trends that might signal emerging problems.
  • Cash Flow Timeline: Daily inflows vs outflows, highlighting periods of potential stress.
  • Maturity Ladder: Distribution of assets and liabilities by maturity bucket, revealing gap positions.

Warning Indicators

The dashboard uses color-coded status indicators: green for healthy metrics, yellow for metrics approaching limits, and red for metrics that breach regulatory thresholds or internal limits.

Tip: Monitor the Liquidity Gap metric closely. A consistently negative gap may indicate structural funding issues that need to be addressed before they become critical.
Note: This dashboard uses illustrative data. Connect it with your bank's actual liquidity data for operational monitoring.
