Credit Risk Scoring Help

What Is This Tool?

The Credit Risk Scoring tool assesses the creditworthiness of a loan portfolio using statistical models. It calculates Probability of Default (PD), Loss Given Default (LGD), and provides an overall risk score to help banks make informed lending decisions.

Key Metrics Explained

  • Overall Risk Score (0-100): A composite score where higher values indicate lower risk. Scores above 70 are considered low risk, 50-70 moderate, and below 50 high risk.
  • Default Probability (PD): The estimated likelihood that a borrower will default within 12 months. Industry average is typically 1-5% for commercial portfolios.
  • Portfolio NPL: Non-Performing Loan ratio, showing the percentage of loans in distress. Below 2% is generally considered healthy.
  • Loan Count: Total number of active loans being analyzed in the portfolio.

Understanding the Charts

  • Default Probability Distribution: Shows how default risk is distributed across the portfolio. A left-skewed distribution (concentrated at low values) indicates a healthy portfolio.
  • Risk Score vs Portfolio Balance: Scatter plot revealing the relationship between loan size and risk level. Look for clusters and outliers.
  • Asset Quality Breakdown: Categorizes loans by quality grade (Pass, Watch, Substandard, Doubtful, Loss) per regulatory classification standards.

Risk Grade Definitions

  • Pass: Loans performing as expected with minimal risk
  • Watch: Loans showing early signs of potential weakness
  • Substandard: Loans with well-defined weaknesses that jeopardize repayment
  • Doubtful: Loans where collection in full is highly questionable
  • Loss: Loans considered uncollectible

Regulatory Context

This tool aligns with Basel III/IV regulatory frameworks for credit risk assessment. The scoring methodology incorporates factors consistent with FDIC and OCC examination guidelines.

Note: This tool provides estimates for analysis purposes. Actual credit decisions should incorporate additional due diligence, market conditions, and regulatory requirements.
